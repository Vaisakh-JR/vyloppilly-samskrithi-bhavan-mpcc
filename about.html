About Us

Kerala is a narrow strip of land on the South West coast of India protected by natural boundaries and blessed with scenic beauty. The uniqueness of the state is the cultural diversity and its vibrance. Due to these reasons, Kerala stands apart from other states. Our art forms range from classical, temple arts, rituals, folk and tribal art forms. This cultural tradition influenced our socio-political history of the state.

Due to the cultural stream rolling, cultural values and art forms are sink into obscurity and all these have to be preserved intact and passed on to the coming generations. This social commitment derived the idea to establish a cultural centre in the heart of Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala’s capital city.

The centre was registered on the 12th of January 1998 under the Charitable Societies Act. The society was named Multi Purpose Cultural Complex Society (MPCCS). Hon. Minister of Culture, Govt. of Kerala serves as the Chairman of the society. Other members are drawn from various spheres like art, culture, bureaucrats, etc., who have accomplished in these areas. The centre came to life at Nalanda, Nanthancode, Thiruvananthapuram in a sprawling 2.06 acre area with the financial assistance and approval from the Government of India. This autonomous body started functioning under the Department of Culture, Government of Kerala.The Multi Purpose Cultural Complex was dedicated to the nation on 7th of January 2001.The centre was named Vyloppilly Samskrithi Bhavan in honour of the great poet Vyloppilli Sreedhara Menon. The most important aim of this institution is to hand over the traditional art forms to the succeeding generations with the help of state-of-the-art technological innovations.

Vyloppilly Sreedhara Menon, who emerged as a poet ahead of his times showed the bare reality of the human life through his literary works. Through his works he touched the human mind and exposed the dark realities in it. He never compromised the values in his life as well as in his works. The famous quote of Vyloppilly Sreedhara Menon - ‘Nenchukeerinjan nerine kattum’ (നെഞ്ചു കീറി ഞാൻ നേരിനെ കാട്ടും) shows the social commitment and his approach towards the humankind. The unparalleled celebrated writer through his poetic works proclaimed the unconditional support and commitment to the marginalized. That is why this cultural complex which was envisioned by the Government as an important cultural centre of Kerala and was named Vyloppilly Samskrithi Bhavan.
